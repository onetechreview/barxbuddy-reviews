Does This Thing REALLY Work?
My BarxBuddy Dog Training Device Review

Rachel Williams

12 February 2021

I tried out the dog-training tool that supposedly puts a stop to annoying barking, unwelcomed jumping, the constant digging, and house-destroying chewing—no painful prong or shock collars needed!

It was another sunny summer day in the suburbs of St. Louis and I was taking my 2-year-old rescue pup, Banjo, on his afternoon walk.

“Walk” would be the nice way to put it.

What really happened was me getting dragged around my neighborhood by my 50-lb lab mutt who tried to chase every squirrel up a tree, bark at every passing dog, and lunge at every jogger running by.

He wasn’t aggressive. Really, he just wanted to play with all the dogs he saw and lick everyone’s face.

I found myself trying to convince my neighbors of this to cover up my pup’s poor behavior. “He just wants to play! He’s still a puppy!”

But if you’ve ever been on the other side of these situations, you can never really be sure if a dog you don’t know is coming at you with a “friendly” lick or an “aggressive” lunge. And as the owner, it feels embarrassing.

Sure, I was tired of being pulled around and feeling out of control. But I was more tired of seeing my neighbors cross to the other side of the street and the mailman hightail it to his truck whenever they saw Banjo and me coming.

My heart was breaking. Banjo was such a sweet boy and just wanted to love up on people. But he’d come from the streets and had zero “formal” training prior to me adopting him.

I certainly wasn’t helping the situation, and I knew it. But I didn’t feel right about using those medieval prong collars or shock devices on my sweet boy that were coming up when I Google searched how to solve this problem!

Then one day, I took Banjo to the local dog park to try and tire him out, hoping that would calm him down a bit.

There were plenty of other “crazy” dogs running around, wrestling, jumping up on the picnic tables, and barking at every new dog that came through the gate.

But in the back corner, I noticed a man playing fetch with his two German shepherds. They were paying attention to his every command and ignoring everything else going on in the park.

I noticed he had a little device in his hand that he occasionally put in front of his dogs, causing them to sit and wait patiently at his feet on command.

I got curious about how this man was commanding total control over these well-behaved dogs, and I approached him to ask his “secret.”

The man introduced himself as Steve, and turns out he was a highly sought-after dog trainer who was regularly recruited by police and service dog centers around the country to train their dogs and instruct their trainers!

If you’re wondering about that little device he was using, it’s called BarxBuddy.

What Is the BarxBuddy?

It’s a tool the size of a small TV remote that uses sound waves to break your dog’s attention and calm them down—PAIN-FREE! (Unlike those shock collars and devices that dig into your puppy’s skin.)

Steve told me about this concept of how he trained all of his dogswith BarxBuddy called, “Train, Treat, Repeat” or TTR.

It works like this…

Anytime your dog does something unruly, you use the BarxBuddy to get his attention. (Your dog can hear the ultrasonic sound waves, but you can’t.)

Then, you give a firm command like “DOWN” and give him a treat once he obeys.

Steve explained that just correcting your dog isn’t enough. You have to reward the good behavior so they know what to do next time. Then you repeat this process of “positive reinforcement” over and over again.

This man had trained hundreds of dogs and said that “Train, Treat, Repeat” was his go-to method for all the dogs he’d worked with.

Still, this guy had been training different breeds of dogs for decades. I thought there had to be more to his method than the simple click of a button. I remained skeptical.

Later that week, I had a few friends over for dinner. Banjo “greeted” each of them at the door with his typical jumping and licking routine. And of course, not to my surprise, it persisted once we’d all sat down at the table.

I told them about my experience at the dog park, meeting Steve, and this magical little device he was using.

Out of nowhere, my best friend said, “Oh, you mean the BarxBuddy? That’s how I trained my dog!”

My eyes lit up. I was bewildered. How was it that I’d never heard of this thing before, and now within a single week a stranger at the dog park and my own best friend were singing its praises?!

I saw over 1,600 positive reviews from happy customers with dogs just like Banjo who had used the BarxBuddy to finally put an end to their excessive barking, jumping, and chewing.

Just like me, many of these people didn’t like the idea of using painful pinching or shocking devices on their fur babies and loved the PAIN-FREE alternative offered from the BarxBuddy.
